> **"The power of taxation, which is expressly granted, is plenary, and is limited only by the requirement that it shall be exercised uniformly throughout the United States."** **

> ... natural rights, so called, are as much subject to taxation as rights of less importance. An excise is not limited to vocations or activities
that may be prohibited altogether. It is not limited to those that are the outcome of a franchise. It extends to vocations or activities pursued as
 of common right. [Steward Machine Co. v. Davis, 301 U.S. 548 (1937)](https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/301/548/)

> Title VIII lays another excise upon employers in addition to the one imposed by Title IX (though with different exemptions). It lays a special income tax upon employees 
to be deducted from their wages and paid by the employers. [Helvering v. Davis, 301 U.S. 619 (1937)](https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/301/619/)

 including senators and representatives and delegates in Congress, when exceeding the rate of six hundred dollars per annum, a duty of three per
centum on the excess above the said six hundred dollars; and it shall be the duty of all paymasters, and all disbursing officers, under the government of the 
United States, or in the employ thereof, when making any payments to officers and persons as aforesaid, or upon settling and adjusting the accounts of such officers
